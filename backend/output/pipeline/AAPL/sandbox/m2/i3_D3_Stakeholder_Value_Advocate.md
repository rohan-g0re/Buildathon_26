You acknowledge this risk in your downsides but dismiss it with "engage proactively"—that's not a mitigation, that's hope. The EU has already shown it will litigate Apple's pricing structures. You propose testing "in geographic markets outside EU regulatory jurisdiction," but Apple's Services business is globally integrated—iCloud, App Store, and subscription services don't neatly segment by geography without creating friction and confusion. A 92% retention rate reflects a *unified* ecosystem experience. The moment you introduce pricing arbitrage (premium tier in the US, value tier in India, restricted tier in the EU), you risk: (a) customer confusion and support burden, (b) cross-border arbitrage and VPN workarounds, and (c) brand damage from perceived regional discrimination. This isn't a "packaging reconfiguration"—it's a fundamental change to how Apple positions its ecosystem. What's your plan for preventing tier leakage and maintaining brand coherence?
